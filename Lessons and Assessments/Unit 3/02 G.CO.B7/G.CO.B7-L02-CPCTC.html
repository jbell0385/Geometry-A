G.CO.B.7-CPCTC
Objectives

I can use Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles Are Congruent (CPCTC) to solve problems involving congruent triangles.

  1. If two triangles are congruent, I can find the missing side lengths and angle measures.
  2. I can use CPCTC in a two-column proof.

Congruence is when two figures have the same shape and size.

  2. Congruent segments have the same length.
  3. Congruent shapes have both congruent angles and congruent segments.

1. Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles Are Congruent (CPCTC)
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles are Congruent (CPCTC) means that if you know two triangles are congruent, then you know all the corresponding sides and angles of the triangles are congruent.

Example 1.1

In the figure, \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle DEF\) are congruent. However, the measure of \(AC\) is missing.

To find the measure of segment \(AC\) you would do the following:

  1. Verify the triangles are congruent. Here it is given.
  2. Since the triangles are congruent, all sides are corresponding sides are congruent according to CPCTC.
  3. The corresponding side to \(AC\) is \(DF\).
  4. \(AC\) is 3.5 cm.

Example 1.2

Say \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle DEF\) are congruent. To find the measure of \(\angle B\) you would do the following:

  1. Verify the triangles are congruent. Here it is given.
  2. Since the triangles are congruent, all sides are corresponding sides are congruent according to CPCTC.
  3. The corresponding angle to \(B\) is \(E\).
  4. \(\angle B\) is \(65^{\circ }\).

2. Using CPCTC in Proofs
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

In many geometry proofs involving triangles, you will often use CPCTC as a tool. Proofs will often follow these steps:

  1. Prove that the triangles are congruent.
    • Usually you will use SSS, ASA, AAS, SAS, or HL.
  2. Since the triangles are congruent, you can use CPCTC for any sides or angles.
  3. Use CPCTC to establish your final proof.

Example 2.1

In the diagram, \(AB\) and \(CD\) bisect each other at \(M\). Prove that \(\angle A \cong \angle B\). The steps to solve this proof are as follows:

  1. First note any corresponding segments or angles in the diagram. For example, \(\angle C\) corresponds to \(\angle D\).
  2. Because \(\angle A\) and \(\angle B\) are corresponding angles in \(\triangle ADM\) and \(\triangle BCM\), show that \(\triangle ADM \cong \triangle BCM\) to prove that \(\angle A \cong \angle B\).

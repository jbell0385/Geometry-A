G.SRT.B.5._L04_SSS_and_SAS_Congruence

Objectives

I can use Side-Side-Side (SSS) and Side-Angle-Side (SAS) Congruence Postulates to identify triangles that are congruent.

  1. I can define and use SSS to identify congruent triangles.
  2. I can define and use SAS to identify congruent triangles.
Prior Knowledge

  2. Corresponding
Congruence
×

Congruence is when two figures have the same shape and size.

  1. Congruent angles are angles that have the same degree measure.
  2. Congruent segments have the same length.
  3. Congruent shapes have both congruent angles and congruent segments.

Corresponding
×
Points, sides, edges, or angles in two or more figures that are images of each other with respect to a sequence of transformations. If two figures are congruent, their corresponding parts are congruent to each other.

1. Side-Side-Side (SSS) Congruence Postulate
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

SSS Postulate

You can prove that two triangles are congruent with the SSS postulate. The following must be true for SSS:

  1. All corresponding sides of the triangles must be congruent.

Example 1.1

SSS

In the figure, notice the following about the triangles:

  1. \(\overline{AB} \cong \overline{DE}\).
  2. \(\overline{BC} \cong \overline{EF}\).
  3. \(\overline{AC} \cong \overline{DF}\).
  4. \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEF\) according to SSS.

Example 1.2

Not SSS

In the figure, notice that one of the corresponding sides are not equal. This is not SSS.

Example 1.3

Identify SSS

At first you may not see that all corresponding sides are congruent in \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle DCB\).

However, the triangles share side \(\overline {CB}\).

Therefore, the triangles are congruent according to SSS.

2. Side-Angle-Side (SAS) Congruence Postulate
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

SAS Postulate

You can prove that two triangles are congruent with the SAS postulate. The following must be true for SAS:

  1. Two corresponding sides are congruent.
  2. The including angles are congruent.

Example 2.1

SAS

In the figure, notice the following about the triangles:

  1. \(\overline{AB} \cong \overline{DE}\).
  2. \(\angle B \cong \angle E\).
  3. \(\overline{BC} \cong \overline{EF}\).
  4. \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEF\) according to SAS.

Example 2.2

Not SAS

In the figure, notice that the congruent angle is not in between the congruent corresponding sides. This is not SAS.

Example 2.3

Identify SAS

At first you may not see that all corresponding sides are congruent in \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle DCB\).

However, the triangles share side \(\overline {CB}\).

Therefore, the triangles are congruent according to SAS.

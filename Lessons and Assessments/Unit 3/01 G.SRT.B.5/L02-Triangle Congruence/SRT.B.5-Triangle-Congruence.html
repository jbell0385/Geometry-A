Congruence is when two figures have the same shape and size.

  1. Congruent angles are angles that have the same degree measure.
  2. Congruent segments have the same length.
  3. Congruent shapes have both congruent angles and congruent segments.

×

When you have two lines crossed by a transversal, Corresponding Angles (CA) are angles on the same side of the transversal and occupy the same relative position.

When the lines are parallel, then the Corresponding Angles are congruent.

Properties of Congruent Triangles

Two triangles are congruent if:

  1. Corresponding side lengths are equal
  2. Corresponding angles are equal

Naming Congruent Triangles

When naming congruent triangles, make sure you match corresponding sides and angles.

For example, \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEF\).

It would be incorrect to write \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle FED\), because the order of the corresponding parts do not match.

Not Congruent

This is an example of \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle DEF\) not being congruent.

Finding Missing Values of Congruent Triangles

If you know \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEF\), then you can find the missing values of \(\triangle DEF\).

  1. Match the letters in the congruency statement.
    1. A=D
    2. B=E
    3. C=F
  2. Pair up corresponding angles
    1. \(\angle A=\angle D=60^{\circ}\)
    2. \(\angle B=\angle E=90^{\circ}\)
    3. \(\angle C=\angle F=30^{\circ}\)
  3. Pair up corresponding sides
    1. \(AB=DE=3\)
    2. \(BC=EF=5\)
    3. \(AC=DF=9\)

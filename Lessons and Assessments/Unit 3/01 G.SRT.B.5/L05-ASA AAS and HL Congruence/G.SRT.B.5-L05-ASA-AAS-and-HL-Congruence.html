G.SRT.B.5-L05-ASA AAS and HL Congruence

Objectives

I can use Angle-Side-Angle (ASA), Angle-Angle-Side (AAS), and Hypotenuse-Leg (HL) Congruence Postulates to identify triangles that are congruent.

  1. I can define and use ASA to identify congruent triangles.
  2. I can define and use AAS to identify congruent triangles.
  3. I can define and use HL to identify congruent triangles.
Prior Knowledge

  2. Corresponding
Congruence
×

Congruence is when two figures have the same shape and size.

  1. Congruent angles are angles that have the same degree measure.
  2. Congruent segments have the same length.
  3. Congruent shapes have both congruent angles and congruent segments.
Close
Corresponding
×
Points, sides, edges, or angles in two or more figures that are images of each other with respect to a sequence of transformations. If two figures are congruent, their corresponding parts are congruent to each other.

1. Angle-Side-Angle (ASA) Congruence Postulate
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

You can prove that two triangles are congruent with the ASA postulate. The following must be true for ASA:

  1. There are two pairs of congruent angles.
  2. The corresponding sides between the angles are congruent.

Example 1.1

ASA

In the figure, notice the following about the triangles:

  1. \(\angle A \cong \angle D\).
  2. \(\overline{AB} \cong \overline{DE}\).
  3. \(\angle B \cong \angle E\).
  4. \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEF\) according to ASA.

Example 1.2

Not ASA

Example 1.3

Identify ASA
  1. \(\angle BAC \cong \angle DCA\)
  2. \(\angle BCA \cong \angle DAC\)
  3. The two triangles share side \(AC\), so that side is congruent to both triangles.
  4. The triangles are congruent according to ASA

2. Angle-Angle-Side (AAS) Congruence Postulate
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

AAS Postulate

You can prove that two triangles are congruent with the AAS postulate. The following must be true for AAS:

  1. Two pairs of corresponding angles are congruent.
  2. A pair of corresponding sides are congruent.
  3. The congruent corresponding sides are not in between the congruent angles.

Example 2.1: AAS

In the figure, notice the following about the triangles:

  1. \(\angle A \cong \angle D\).
  2. \(\angle B \cong \angle E\).
  3. \(\overline{BC} \cong \overline{EF}\).
  4. \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEF\) according to AAS.

Example 2.2: Not AAS

In the figure, notice that there are two pairs of congruent sides and 1 pair of congruent angles. This is not AAS.

Example 2.3: Identify AAS

  1. \(\angle B \cong \angle D\)
  2. \(\angle BAC \cong \angle DCA\)
  3. The two triangles share side \(AC\), so that side is congruent to both triangles.
  4. The triangles are congruent according to AAS.

Self-Check 2.1

Are the triangles congruent according to the AAS congruence postulate?

Answer
Answer: Self-Check 2.1

Self-Check 2.2

Are the triangles congruent according to AAS congruence postulate?

Answer
Answer: Self-Check 2.2
×

\(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle DCB\) are congruent.

  1. \(\angle DBC \cong \angle ACB\)
  2. \(\angle A \cong \angle D\)
  3. Both triangles share side \(BC\). Therefore that side is congruent.

3. Hypotenuse-Leg (HL) Congruence Postulate
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

HL Postulate

You can prove that two triangles are congruent with the HL postulate. The following must be true for HL:

  1. The triangles must have a right (\(90^{\circ}\)) angle.
  2. The hypotenuse of the triangles are congruent.
  3. The triangles have to have a leg (the sides that aren’t the hypotenuse) that are congruent.

Example 3.1

HL

In the figure, notice the following about the triangles:

  1. \(\angle B\) and \(\angle E\) are right angles.
  2. \(\overline{AC} \cong \overline{DF}\).
  3. \(\overline{BC} \cong \overline{EF}\).
  4. \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEF\) according to HL.

Example 3.2

Not HL

In the figure, notice that the angle is not a right angle. This is not HL.

Example 3.3

Identify HL
  1. \(\angle ABC\) and \(\angle DCB\) are right angles.
  2. The two triangles share side \(BC\), so that side is congruent to both triangles.
  3. The triangles are congruent according to HL.

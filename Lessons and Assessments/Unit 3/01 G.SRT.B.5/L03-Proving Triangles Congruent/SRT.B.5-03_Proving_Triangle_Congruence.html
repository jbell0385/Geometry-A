SRT.B.5 Proving Triangle Congruence

Objectives

I can create formal proofs to prove two triangles are congruent.

  1. I can create formal triangle congruence proofs using SSS.
  2. I can create formal triangle congruence proofs using SAS.
  3. I can create formal triangle congruence proofs using ASA.
  4. I can create formal triangle congruence proofs using AAS.
  5. I can create formal triangle congruence proofs using HL.

Two-Column Proof
×

A two-column proof is a structure for creating geometric proofs. A two column table is created with statements on the left side and reasons on the right side.

Two-Column Proofs Example

Given: \(line\ a\parallel line\ b\)
\(\angle 1 = 150^{\circ}\)

Prove: \(\angle 6 30^{\circ}\)

  1. Line \(a\) is parallel to line \(b\). That is given.
  2. Angle 1 equals \(150^{\circ}\)
  3. Since we know that the lines are parallel, then we can use same-side interior angles to determine that angle 6 is \(30^{\circ}\)

SSS Congruence Postulate
×

The Side-Side-Side (SSS) Congruence Postulate states that if two triangles have 3 pairs of corresponding sides that are congruent, then the triangles are congruent.

Close
SAS Congruence Postulate
×

The Side-Angle-Side (SAS) Congruence Postulate states that if two triangles have 2 pairs of corresponding sides that are congruent and the included angles are congruent, then the triangles are congruent.

Close
ASA Congruence Postulate
×

The Angle-Side-Angle (ASA) Congruence Postulate states that if two triangles have 1 pair of corresponding sides that are congruent and 2 pairs of congruent angles that include the congruent sides, then the triangles are congruent.

Close
AAS Congruence Postulate
×

The Angle-Angle-Side (AAS) Congruence Postulate states that if two triangles have 2 pairs of congruent angles and 1 non-included congruent sides, then the triangles are congruent.

Close
HL Congruence Postulate
×

The Hypotenuse-Leg (HL) Congruence Postulate states that if two triangles are right triangles, the hypotenuses are congruent, and one other corresponding leg pair is congruent, then the triangles are congruent.

1. Formal Proofs With SSS
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

In a formal proof using SSS you try to do the following:

  1. Identify any given information.
  2. Prove that all corresponding sides are congruent (SSS).
    1. Definition of Midpoint
    2. Reflexive Property
  3. SSS proves that the triangles are congruent.

Example 1.1

Proofs with SSS

Given: \(\overline {AB} \cong \overline {DB}\), \(\overline {AC} \cong \overline {DC}\).

Prove: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DBC\)

The steps to solve are as follows:

  1. Given: \(\overline {AB} \cong \overline {DB}\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {AB}\) and \(\overline {DC}\).
  2. Given: \(\overline {AC} \cong \overline {DC}\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {AC}\) and \(\overline {DC}\).
  3. Reflexive property: \(\overline {BC} \cong \overline {BC}\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {BC}\)
  4. SSS Postulate: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DBC\).

Example 1.2

Midpoint SSS Proof Problems

Given: \(\overline {AB} \cong \overline {DB}\), \(C\) is the midpoint of \(\overline {AD}\).

Prove: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DBC\)

The steps to solve are as follows:

  1. Given: \(\overline {AB} \cong \overline {DB}\)
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {AB}\) and \(\overline {DB}\).
  2. Given: \(C\) is the midpoint of \(\overline {AD}\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {AC}\) and \(\overline {DC}\).
  3. Definition of midpoint: \(\overline {AC} \cong \overline {DC}\).
  4. Reflexive property: \(\overline {BC} \cong \overline {BC}\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {BC}\)
  5. SSS Postulate: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DBC\).

Self-Check 1.1

Given: \(\overline {AB} \cong \overline {DC}\), \(\overline {AC} \cong \overline {DB}\).

Prove: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DCB\)

2. Formal Proofs With SAS
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

In a formal proof using SAS you try to do the following:

  1. Identify any given information.
  2. Prove that two pairs of corresponding sides are congruent.
    1. Definition of Midpoint
    2. Reflexive Property
  3. Prove that the included angles are congruent.
    1. Angle Bisector
    2. Vertical Angles
    3. Definition of Perpendicular
    4. Definition of Parallel
  4. SAS proves that the triangles are congruent.

Example 2.1

Vertical Angles

Given: \(\overline {AC} \cong \overline {DC}\), \(\overline {BC} \cong \overline {EC}\).

Prove: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEC\)

The steps to solve are as follows:

  1. Given: \(\overline {AC} \cong \overline {DC}\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {AC}\) and \(\overline {DC}\).
  2. Given: \(\overline {BC} \cong \overline {EC}\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {BC}\) and \(\overline {EC}\).
  3. Vertical angles: \(\angle {ACB} \cong \angle {DCE}\).
      4. Put tick marks on \(\angle {C}\)
  4. SAS Postulate: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEC\).

Example 2.2

Angle Bisector

Given: \(\overline {AB} \cong \overline {DB}\), \(\overline {BC}\) bisects \(\angle ABD\).

Prove: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DBC\)

The steps to solve are as follows:

  1. Given: \(\overline {AB} \cong \overline {DB}\)
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {AB}\) and \(\overline {DB}\).
  2. Given: \(\overline {BC}\) bisects \(\angle ABD\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\angle ABC\) and \(\angle DBC\).
  3. Definition of angle bisector: \(\angle ABC \cong \angle DBC\).
  4. Reflexive property: \(\overline {BC} \cong \overline {BC}\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {BC}\)
  5. SAS Postulate: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DBC\).

4. Formal Proofs With AAS
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

In a formal proof using AAS you try to do the following:

  1. Identify any given information.
  2. Prove that two pairs of corresponding angles are congruent.
    1. Angle Bisector
    2. Vertical Angles
    3. Definition of Perpendicular
    4. Definition of Parallel
  3. Prove that the non-included side pair is congruent.
    1. Definition of Midpoint
    2. Reflexive Property
  4. AAS proves that the triangles are congruent.

Example 4.1

Parallel Lines

Given: \(\overline {AC} \cong \overline {DC}\), \(\overline {AB} \parallel \overline {DE}\).

Prove: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEC\)

The steps to solve are as follows:

  1. Given: \(\overline {AC} \cong \overline {DC}\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {AC}\) and \(\overline {DC}\).
  2. Given: \(\overline {AB} \parallel \overline {DE}\).
  3. \(\angle BAC \cong \angle EDC\) using alternate interior angles.
  4. \(\angle ABC \cong \angle DEC\) using alternate interior angles.
  5. \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DEC\) using the AAS Congruence Postulate.

Example 4.2

Angle Bisector

Given: \(\angle A \cong \angle D\), \(\overline {BC}\) bisects \(\angle ABD\).

Prove: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DBC\)

The steps to solve are as follows:

  1. Given: \(\angle A \cong \angle D\)
    1. Put tick marks on \(\angle A\) and \(\angle D\).
  2. Given: \(\overline {BC}\) bisects \(\angle ABD\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\angle ABC\) and \(\angle DBC\).
  3. Definition of angle bisector: \(\angle ABC \cong \angle DBC\).
  4. Reflexive property: \(\overline {BC} \cong \overline {BC}\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {BC}\)
  5. AAS Postulate: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DBC\).

5. Formal Proofs With HL
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

In a formal proof using HL you try to do the following:

  • Identify any given information.
  • Prove that the triangle is a right triangle (\(90^{\circ}\)).
    • Definition of Perpendicular
  • Prove that the hypotenuses are congruent.
    • Definition of Midpoint
    • Reflexive Property
  • Prove that a pair of corresponding legs are congruent.
    • Definition of Midpoint
    • Reflexive Property
  • HL proves that the triangles are congruent.

Example 5.1

Perpendicular

Given: \(\overline {AB} \cong \overline {DB}\), \(\overline {BC} \perp \overline {AD}\).

Prove: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DBC\)

The steps to solve are as follows:

  1. Given: \(\overline {AB} \cong \overline {DB}\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {AB}\) and \(\overline {DB}\).
  2. Given: \(\overline {BC} \perp \overline {AD}\).
    1. Mark \(\angle ACB\) and \(\angle DCB\) as \(90^{\circ}\).
  3. Definition of Perpendicular: \(\angle ACB\) and \(\angle DCB\) are right angles.
  4. Reflexive Property: \(\overline {BC} \cong \overline {BC}\).
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {BC}\)
  5. HL Postulate: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DBC\).

Example 5.2

Angle Bisector

Given: \(m\angle ABC=90^{\circ} , m\angle DCB=90^{\circ} , \overline {AC} \cong \overline {DB}\)

Prove: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DCB\)

The steps to solve are as follows:

  1. You are given that two angles measure\(90^{\circ}\)
    1. Mark \(\angle ABC\) and \(\angle DCB\) as \(90^{\circ}\).
  2. To use HL, you must state that the two angles are right.
    1. Put tick marks on \(\overline {AC}\) and \(\overline {DB}\).
  3. You are given that \(\overline {AC} \cong \overline {DB}\)
    1. Label the congruent sides with tick marks.
  4. \(\overline {BC}\) is used in both triangles so \(\overline {BC} \cong \overline {BC}\) by the Reflexive Property of Congruence.
  5. The corresponding hypotenuses and legs are congruent in both right triangles so \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DCB\) by the HL Congruence Postulate.

Self-Check 5.1

Given: \(\overline {AB} \perp \overline {BC}\), \(\overline {DC} \perp \overline {BC}\), \(\overline {AC} \cong \overline {DB}\)

Prove: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DCB\)

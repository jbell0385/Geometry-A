Summary Power Standards Learning Targets

Unit 3 focuses on triangles. You will start the unit by learning two important properties for all triangles. You will then apply the properties throughout the remainder of the unit and course. The final lessons will have you prove triangles and their individual parts congruent.

The three main power standards that Unit 3 will focus on and will formally assess are:

  1. GMD.B.6: Verify experimentally that in a triangle, angles opposite longer sides are larger, sides opposite larger angles are longer, and the sum of any two side lengths is greater than the remaining side length. Then apply these relationships to solve real world and mathematical problems.
  2. SRT.B.5: Use congruence criteria for triangles to solve problems and to prove relationships in geometric figures.
  3. CO.B.7: Use the definition of congruence in terms of rigid motions to show that two triangles are congruent if and only if corresponding pairs of sides and corresponding pairs of angles are congruent.

Learning Target 1

I can use congruence postulates of triangles to prove various properties of geometric figures.

Learning Target 2

I can apply transformations to triangles and know they are congruent by listing corresponding pairs of sides and corresponding pairs of angles using congruence statements.

Learning Target 3

I can use the Triangle Inequality Theorem to determine if three side lengths can determine a triangle and find possible values for a side of a triangle using real-world applications.

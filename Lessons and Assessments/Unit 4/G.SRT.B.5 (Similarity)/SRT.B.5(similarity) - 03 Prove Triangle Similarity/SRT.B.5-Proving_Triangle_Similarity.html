SRT.B.5: Proving Triangle Similarity

Objectives

I can prove two triangles are similar

  1. I can prove two triangles are similar with AA Similarity Postulate.
  2. I can prove two triangles are similar with SAS Similarity Postulate.
  3. I can prove two triangles are similar with SSS Similarity Postulate.
Prior Knowledge
  1. AA Similarity Postulate
  2. SAS Similarity Postulate
  3. SSS Similarity Postulate
AA Similarity Postulate
×

Angle-Angle (AA) Similarity Postulate has the following:

  1. The two triangles must have two pairs of congruent corresponding angles.
  2. \(\angle A\cong \angle A'\)
  3. \(\angle B\cong \angle B'\)
  4. \(\triangle ABC\sim \triangle A'B'C'\)
Close
SAS Similarity Postulate
×

Side-Angle-Side (SAS) Similarity Postulate has the following:

  1. The two triangles must have two pairs of corresponding sides with the same ratio.
    • \(\frac{6}{3}=2\)
    • \(\frac{8}{4}=2\)
  2. The corresponding included angles must be congruent.
Close
SSS Similarity Postulate
×

Side-Side-Side (SSS) Similarity Postulate has the following:

  1. The two triangles must have three pairs of corresponding sides with the same ratio.
  2. \(\frac{A}{A'}=\frac{B}{B'}=\frac{C}{C'}\)
Close
1. Proving Triangles with AA Similarity Postulate

To prove triangles similar using the AA Similarity Postulate, you will apply previous learned theorems and properties. Review these important terms:

  • Same Side Interior Angles
  • Vertical Angles
Congruence
×

Congruence is when two figures have the same shape and size.

  1. Congruent angles are angles that have the same degree measure.
  2. Congruent segments have the same length.
  3. Congruent shapes have both congruent angles and congruent segments.
Close
Reflexive Property
×

Reflexive property of congruence is when two figures share the same side length, then that side length is congruent in both triangles.

  1. Two triangles \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle CDA\).
  2. \(\overline{AC}\cong\overline{AC}\).
Close
Corresponding Angles
×

When you have two lines crossed by a transversal, corresponding angles (CA) are angles on the same side of the transversal and occupy the same relative position.

When the lines are parallel, then the corresponding angles are congruent.

Close
Alternate Interior Angles
×

When you have two lines crossed by a transversal, alternate interior angles (AIA) are angles on the inner side of two lines, but on opposite sides of the transversal.

When the lines are parallel, then the alternate interior angles are congruent.

Close
Alternate Exterior Angles
×

When you have two lines crossed by a transversal, alternate exterior angles (AEA) are angles on the outer side of the two lines, but on opposite sides of the transversal.

When the lines are parallel, then the alternate exterior angles are congruent.

Close
Same-Side Interior Angles
×

When you have two lines crossed by a transversal, same-side interior angles (SSIA) are angles on the inner side of the two lines, and on the same side of the transversal.

When the lines are parallel, then the sum of the same-side interior angles equals \(180^{\circ}\).

Close
Vertical Angles
×

When two straight lines intersect, the pairs of oppposite angles are congruent.

Look for vertical angles in shapes, such as two connected triangles.

Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

To prove that two triangles are similar using SAS \(\sim\), you want to use what you are given in the problem and your knowledge of geometry in order to prove that two pairs of corresponding sides of a triangle have the same scale factor and their included angles are congruent.

  • Reflexive Property of Congruence
  • Corresponding Angles

  • Vertical Angles

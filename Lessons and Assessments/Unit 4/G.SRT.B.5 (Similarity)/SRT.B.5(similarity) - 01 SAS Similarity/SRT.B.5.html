Side-Angle-Side (SAS) Postulate

Side-angle-side (SAS) similarity states that if two pairs of corresponding side lengths have a common scale factor and their included angles are congruent, then the triangles are similar.

Not SAS

An important situation to watch out for is the side-side-angle (SSA) scenario. In these situations, the angle is not between the relevant sides. SSA is not the same as SAS. Also, SSA is not enough information to conclude two triangles are similar.

Example 1.1

Are \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle A'B'C'\) similar according to SAS?

  1. Find the ratio between side \(AC\) and side \(A'C'\).
    The ratio in this example is 3.
  2. Find the ratio between side \(BC\) and side \(B'C'\).
    The ratio in this example is 3.
  3. Verify that the angles are congruent.
    In this situation the angles are both \(48^{\circ}\).
  4. Since the sides have a common ratio of 3 and the included angles are congruent, the two triangles are congruent according to SAS.

Example 1.2

Are \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle A'B'C'\) similar according to SAS?

  1. The first thing to notice is that the triangles form a vertical angle at point \(C\).
    Therefore, \(\angle ACB\) \(\cong\) \(\angle A'C'B'\).
  2. Find the ratio between side \(AC\) and side \(A'C'\).
    The ratio in this example is 3.
  3. Find the ratio between side \(BC\) and side \(B'C'\).
    The ratio in this example is 2.
  4. Verify that the angles are congruent.
    In this situation the angles are both \(48^{\circ}\).
  5. Since the sides do not have a common ratio, \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle A'B'C'\) are not similar according to SAS.

Example 1.3

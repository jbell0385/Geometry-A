Adding Fractions

To add fractions together do the following:

  1. Find the common denominator (bottom number).
  2. Multiply each fraction by the other fraction's denominator
  3. Add the numerators (top number)

Subtracting Fractions

To add fractions together do the following:

  1. Find the common denominator (bottom number).
  2. Multiply each fraction by the other fraction's denominator
  3. Subtract the numerators (top number)

Multiplying Fractions

To add fractions together do the following:

  1. Multiply the numerators (top number) together.
  2. Multiply the denominators (bottom number) together.

Dividing Fractions

To add fractions together do the following:

  1. Use the reciprocal (flip upside down) of the second fraction
  2. Multiply the numerators
  3. Multiply the denominators

A ratio is the comparison of how much there is of one item compared to another item. For example, in a bag of candy, there may be 3 blue candies for every 2 red candies.

Example 1: Ratio Between Two Colors

Example 2: Part to the Whole

If you had a pizza with 12 slices and you ate 7 pieces. The ratio of slices you ate to the whole would be \(7/12\)

1. Using Ratios to Partition Segments
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

You can partition a segment to produce a given ratio. The steps to do this are as follows:

  1. Find the x distance between the endpoints.
  2. Find the y distance between the endpoints.
  3. Multiply those distances by the ratio.
  4. Add the endpoint offset

Example 1.1

Line segment \(AB\) has the coordinates \(A(-7,4)\) and \(B(8,9)\).
Find the coordinates of point C, which divides the segment into a ratio of \(3:2\)

Step 1: Find the X and Y Distances

Subtract the two endpoints from each other to find the x and y distances.

Step 2: Multiply by the Ratio

Since the ratio is \(3:2\) that means the total is 5 parts. Next, multiply the x and y distances by one of the partitions ratios.

Step 3: Add the Offset

The numbers in step 2 need to be offset by the starting coordinate. In this situation the starting coordinate is \((-7,4)\). This will give you the coordinate of the point that partitions the segment in the given ratio.

Result

Point C is \((2,7)\). This partitions segment \(AB\) into the proportion of \(3:2\).

Self-Check 1.1:

Segment \(AB\) has the coordinates \(A(-1,2)\) and \(B(7,8)\). Find the coordinates of point \(C\), which divides the segment into a ratio of \(1:3\).

Answer
Self-Check 1
×

The value of coordinate C is \(\left( \dfrac {5} {3},4\right) \)

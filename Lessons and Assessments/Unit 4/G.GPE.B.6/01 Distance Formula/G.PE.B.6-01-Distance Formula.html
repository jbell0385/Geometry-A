  1. Pythagorean Theorem
Pythagorean Theorem
×

The Pythagorean Theorem is used to find missing side lengths in a right triangle. It states that the sum of the squared lengths of the legs is equal to the squared length of the hypotenuse. In an equation, this looks like:

\[leg^{2}+leg^{2}=hypotenuse^{2}\] If you use variables for the sides, then the equation becomes: \[a^{2}+b^{2}=c^{2}\]

Finding Missing Sides of Right Triangles

Find the missing side. Round to the hundredths place if necessary.

Steps to solve:

  1. Write down the Pythagorean Theorem formula \(a^{2}+b^{2}=c^{2}\).
  2. Substitute in values for the sides you are given.
  3. Solve the equation for the missing side.

1. Distance of Horizontal and Vertical Coordinates
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

Horizontal Coordinates

If you have two graphed points that are horizontal from each other, you can find the distance between the two points by counting the horizontal spaces between the points.

Vertical Coordinates

If you have two graphed points that are vertical from each other, you can find the distance between the two points by counting the vertical spaces between the points.

Example 1.1: Horizontal Coordinates

Coordinate \(A\) is at \((-2,4)\). Coordinate \(B\) is at \((3,4)\). What is the distance between the two coordinates.

Count the points between \(A\) and \(B\). In this problem, there are 5 points, so the distance between \(A\) and \(B\) is 5.

Example 1.2: Vertical Coordinates

Coordinate \(A\) is at \((-2,4)\). Coordinate \(B\) is at \((-2,-3)\). What is the distance between the two coordinates.

Count the points between \(A\) and \(B\). In this problem, there are 7 points, so the distance between \(A\) and \(B\) is 7.

2. Distance Between Diagonal Coordinates
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

With points that are diagonal from each other on a graph, you must use the Pythagorean Theorem. You will form a triangle with the two points and then use the vertical and horizontal distances in the Pythagorean Theorem.

The general process is as follows:

  1. Form a triangle by drawing a vertical line through one point and a horizontal line through the other point.
  2. Next, find the lengths of the legs of the triangle.
  3. Then, substitute the lengths of the legs of the triangle into the Pythagorean Theorem.
  4. Solve for the missing value \(C\) and this will be the diagonal distance between the two points.

Example 2.1: Diagonal Points

You have two points \(R(-3,-3)\) and \(Q(-1,3)\). What is the distance between the two points?

  1. Draw a vertical line through point \(B\).
  2. Draw a horizontal line through point \(A\).
  3. Draw a line through points \(A\) and \(B\).
  4. Find the distances of the vertical and horizontal legs.
  5. Plug those numbers into the Pythagorean Theorem.
  6. Solve for \(C\), which is the distance between points \(A\) and \(B\).

3. Distance Formula
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

The distance formula allows you to find the distance between two points without using a graph.

The distance formula is as follows: \[\sqrt {\left( y_{2}-y_{1}\right) ^{2}+\left( x_{2}-x_{1}\right) ^{2}}\]

Example 3.1: Distance Formula

Find the distance between the points \(A(-2,4)\) and \(B(3,1)\).

The steps to solve are as follows:

  1. Label the coordinates as \(x_1\), \(y_1\), \(x_2\), and \(y_2\).
  2. Substitute the coordinate values into the distance formula.
  3. Solve the equation.
  4. The value is your distance between the two points. The distance value in this example is 5.83.

Rigid transformations are transformations that:

  • Keep the size constant
  • Keep angle measures constant (which keeps the shape the same) Reflections, Rotations and Translations are Rigid Transformations. Dilations are not rigid because they change the size of a figure.

A proportion is an equation that sets two equal ratios in fraction form equal to each other. The two ratios must be equal to create a proportion. If two values have a proportional relationship, both values must be multiplied by the same scale factor as each other.

These two ratios are proportional because they are equal in value. When simplified, the ratios show that they both have the same value.

1. What is a Dilation?
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

Dilation Definition

A dilation is when figures change size proportionally, with a scale factor, but remain the same shape.

The first figure is a dilation of a water buffalo. The second figure of the water buffalo is not a dilation because the buffalo does not keep its original shape.

Types of Dilation

Enlargement

A dilation that "grows" the image.

When an image is larger than its pre-image, then it is a dilation called an enlargement.

Reduction

A dilation that "shrinks" the image.

When an image is smaller than its pre-image, then it is a dilation called a reduction.

2. Dilation Scale Factor
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

A scale factor is the ratio of two corresponding lengths of an image to its pre-image, usually written as a fraction. To find the scale factor, find the ratio between the corresponding side lengths.

\(\dfrac {image}{pre-image}\)

Note the following when you find the scale factor:

  • Enlargement: The scale factor will be greater than 1.
  • Reduction: The scale factor will be less than 1.

In Geometry, the scale factor is usually noted with the symbol k.

Example 2.1

In order to find the scale factor, do the following:

  1. Find the width of ABCD. The width is 9.
  2. Find the width of A'B'C'D'. The width is 3.
  3. Find the height of A'B'C'D'. The height is 6.
  4. The scale factor is \(\dfrac {1}{3}\).

Objectives

I can use the Angle-Angle (AA) Similarity Postulate to prove triangles are similar and to solve problems.

  1. I can demonstrate that two triangles are similar by comparing all the angles and sides.
  2. I can prove triangles are similar by using the Angle-Angle (AA) Similarity Postulate.

When you have two lines crossed by a transversal, Corresponding Angles (CA) are angles on the same side of the transversal and occupy the same relative position.

When the lines are parallel, then the Corresponding Angles are congruent.

1. Similar Triangles
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

Triangles are similar when their corresponding angles have equal measure and their corresponding sides are proportional. In other words, they have the same shape, but different sizes.

  1. \(\frac{4}{2}=2,\ \frac{10}{5}=2,\ \frac{6}{3}=2\).
  2. All corresponding angles are congruent.

Example 1.1: Similar

In order to see if \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle DEF\) are similar complete the following:

  1. Check that the corresponding angles are congruent.
    • \(\angle A\) is congruent to \(\angle D\)
    • \(\angle B\) is congruent to \(\angle E\)
    • \(\angle C\) is congruent to \(\angle F\)
  2. Check that the corresponding sides all have the same ratio.
    • \(\frac{AB}{DE}\) has a ratio of \(\frac{1}{2}\).
    • \(\frac{AC}{DF}\) has a ratio of \(\frac{1}{2}\).
    • \(\frac{BC}{EF}\) has a ratio of \(\frac{1}{2}\).
  3. Since the angles are congruent and all the sides have the same ratio, then \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle DEF\) are similar.

Example 1.2: Not Similar

These triangles are not similar.

  1. \(\frac{DF}{AC} = \frac{3}{4}\)
  2. \(\frac{DE}{AB} = \frac{2}{3}\)
  3. \(\frac{EF}{BC} = \frac{5}{7}\)

Since the ratios of the corresponding sides are different, then the triangles are not similar.

2. Angle-Angle Similarity Postulate
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

The Angle-Angle (AA) Similarity Postulate states that if two angles in a triangle are congruent to two angles in another, then the triangles are similar.

Example 2.1: Check the Third Angle

Decide if the triangles are similar by AA. If so, write a similarity statement.

You may not think these two triangles are similar, because the two pairs of angles given are not congruent. However, you must check the third angle of the triangles.

  1. \(m\angle N=m\angle K=55^{\circ}\)
  2. Use the Triangle Angle Sum Theorem on \(\triangle MNE\).
  3. \(m\angle E=m\angle P=53^{\circ}\).
  4. Since two pairs of corresponding angles are congruent, then the triangles are similar.
  5. Similarity statement: \(\triangle MNE\sim\triangle OKP\).

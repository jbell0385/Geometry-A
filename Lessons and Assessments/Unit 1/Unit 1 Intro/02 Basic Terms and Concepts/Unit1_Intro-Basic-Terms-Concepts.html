Undefined Terms

Undefined terms are the three major building blocks of geometry that cannot be explained or created using other figures. Examine the terms with their images and naming conventions in the table below.

Term and Definition Diagram Ways to Name
A point ...
  • is a specific location.
  • has no dimension.
  • is shown by a dot.
 A single capital letter.
In this example: P.
A line ...
  • is a straight path without any thickness.
  • has one dimension because it can only travels on one path.
  • has an infinite amount of points that extend forever.
  1. Only two points with a two-way arrow above: \(\overleftrightarrow {CD}, \overleftrightarrow {DC} ;\)
  2. A lowercase cursive letter: line \(m\)
    Note: \(m\) is not a point and therefore must be used alone to name.
A plane ...
  • is any flat surface without any thickness.
  • has two dimensions because it can travel on paths front to back and side to side.
  • extends forever in every direction.
  1. Any three points: plane \(TWV\);
  2. One capital cursive letter: plane \(Q\) Note: \(Q\) is not a point and therefore must be alone to name.

The branch of mathematics that studies points, lines and planes is called geometry.

Defined Terms

Defined terms are created from undefined terms.
Below are some common defined terms often used.

Term and Definition Diagram Ways to Name
A line segment ...
  • is part of a line.
  • has two endpoints or points that complete the path.
 Only two points with a line above (using no arrows): \(\overline{DE},\ \overline{ED}\) ; Note: A cursive letter can NOT be used for segments.
A ray ...
  • has one endpoint.
  • extends forever in the opposite direction
 Only two points with a line above (using one arrow above point closest to arrow in diagram): \(\overrightarrow{TM},\ \overrightarrow{MT}\); Note: A cursive letter can NOT be used for rays.
An angle ...
  • is made of two rays called the sides.
  • are attached a point called the vertex.
  1. Use an angle symbol with only the vertex: \(\angle M\);
  2. Use an angle symbol with all THREE points: \(\angle CMT\) or \(\angle TMC\). Note: The vertex MUST be in the middle. If two or more angles are attached, you must use all three points to name;
  3. Use an angle symbol with the number given: \(\angle 2\).

Angle Measurement

Angles are used often in geometry. Just like segments, angles can be measured. The two units of measurement for angles are degrees and radians. For now, we will focus on angles with degrees. Examine the diagram to the right.

In geometry, you write the measurement of the angle in the interior of the angle, the area between the rays and close to the vertex. Here, the measurement of \(\angle YXZ\) is \(37^{\circ}\) and the mathematical way to state this is to use a cursive \(m\) for the word measurement. Therefore in shorthand:

\(m\angle YXZ=37^{\circ}\)

Review the types of angle classifications in the table below.

Angle Classification Definition Diagram
Acute \(\gt 0^{\circ }\) and \(\lt 90^{\circ }\) or \(0\lt x \lt 90\)
Right Exactly \(90^{\circ }\) (must use a box to show \(90^{\circ }\) in diagrams)
Obtuse \(\gt 90^{\circ }\) and \(\lt 180^{\circ }\) or \(90\lt x \lt 180\)
Straight Exactly \(180°\)

Summary Power Standards Learning Targets

Unit 1 focuses on rigid transformations which are:

  • Reflections
  • Translations
  • Rotations
Geometry begins with rigid transformations to help you eventually prove that two figures have the same shape and size.
You will start by deciding which rigid motion is displayed and then you will be able to complete a series of transformations on a figure. You will also develop multiple definitions for rigid motions to better understand how to apply them to real life situations.

The three main power standards that Unit 1 will focus on and formally assess are:

  1. CO.A.3: Given a rectangle, parallelogram, trapezoid, or regular polygon, describe the rotations and reflections that carry it onto itself.
  2. CO.A.4: Develop definitions of rotations, reflections, and translations in terms of angles, circles, perpendicular lines, parallel lines, and line segments.
  3. CO.A.5: Given a geometric figure and a rotation, reflection, or translation, draw the transformed figure using resources such as graph paper, tracing paper, or geometry software. Specify a sequence of transformations that will carry a given figure onto another.

Learning Target 1

I can identify all degrees of rotation and lines of reflection that carry a figure onto itself symmetrically.

Learning Target 2

I can identify and explain transformations such as rotations, reflections, and translations in terms of angles, circles, perpendicular lines, parallel lines, and line segments.

Learning Target 3

I can perform and identify a sequence of transformations on a given image.

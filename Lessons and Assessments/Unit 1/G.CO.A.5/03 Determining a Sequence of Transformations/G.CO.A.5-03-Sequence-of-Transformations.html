Corresponding
×
Points, sides, edges, or angles in two or more figures that are images of each other with respect to a sequence of transformations. If two figures are congruent, their corresponding parts are congruent to each other.
Close
Translation
×
Translation is a type of rigid transformation in which an object slides on a graph. It does not change its shape or size.
Close
Rotation
×
A shape has rotational symmetry when it can be rotated less than \(360°\) about a point, called the center or rotation, and appears to not change.
Close
Reflection
×
A transformation across a line that produces a mirror image of the original (preimage) shape. The reflection is called the image of the original figure. The line is called a line of symmetry.
Close
1. Corresponding Parts Same Direction - Translation
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

If the corresponding parts of the pre-image and the image are oriented in the same direction and the two figures are not rotated, then you can use a translation to map one figure onto the other.

Example 1.1

What transformation maps \(\triangle ABC\) to \(\triangle A'B'C'\)?

To solve this problem you perform the following:

  1. \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle A'B'C'\) have corresponding parts oriented in the same direction and are not rotated. Therefore, you need to perform a translation.
  2. Select the vector tool.
  3. Draw a vector with the magnitude you need \((x+3, y+2)\).
  4. Select the "Translate by Vector Tool".
  5. Click on the triangle and then the vector line to create the transformation.

2. Corresponding Parts Same Direction - Rotation
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

If the corresponding parts of the pre-image and the image are oriented in the same direction and the two figures are rotated, then you can use a rotation to map one figure onto the other.

Example 2.1

What transformations maps \(\triangle ABC\) to \(\triangle A'B'C'\)?

To solve this problem you perform the following:

  1. \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle A'B'C'\) have corresponding parts oriented in the same direction and are rotated. Therefore, you need to perform a rotation.
  2. Place the center of rotation at \((1,1)\).
  3. Select the "Rotate Around Point" tool.
  4. Rotate \(\triangle ABC\) by \(90^{\circ}\) to map \(\triangle ABC\) to \(\triangle A'B'C'\).

3. Corresponding Parts Opposite Direction - Reflection
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

If the corresponding parts of the pre-image and the image are oriented in the opposite direction, then you can use a reflection to map one figure onto the other.

Example 3.1

What transformation maps \(\triangle ABC\) to \(\triangle A'B'C'\)?

To solve this problem you perform the following:

  1. \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle A'B'C'\) have corresponding parts oriented in the opposite direction. Therefore, you need to perform a reflection.
  2. Place the line of reflection \(x=0\).
  3. Reflect \(\triangle ABC\) over \(x=0\) to map \(\triangle ABC\) to \(\triangle A'B'C'\).

4. Determine a Sequence of Transformations
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

In order to map a pre-image onto an image, often you have to use a sequence of transformations. For example, you may have to use both a translation and a reflection.

Example 4.1

What transformation maps \(\triangle ABC\) to \(\triangle A'B'C'\)?

To solve this problem you perform the following:

  1. There are three things you need to notice:
    • \(\triangle A'B'C'\) is translated
    • \(\triangle A'B'C'\) is rotated
    • \(\triangle A'B'C'\) is reflected
  2. First, translate \(\triangle ABC\) by \((x+4,y+2)\).
  3. Second, rotate \(\triangle ABC\) \(90^{\circ}\) about \((1,4)\).
  4. Lastly, reflect \(\triangle ABC\) over the line \(x=3\).

What transformation maps \(\triangle ABC\) to \(\triangle A'B'C'\)?

Answer

Self-Check 4.1
×

One potential answer is \(r_{x=-2} \circ R_{(90^{\circ},(-2,2))} \circ T_{-3,1}\).

In other words you can say:

  1. First, translate \(ABC\) by \((x-3,y+1)\).
  2. Next, rotate by \(90^{\circ}\) about \((-2,2)\).
  3. Lastly, reflect over the line \(x=-2\).

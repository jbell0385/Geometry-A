Two-Column Proofs
×

A two-column proof is a structure for creating geometric proofs. A two column table is created with statements on the left side and reasons on the right side.

Two-Column Proofs Example

Given: \(a||b,\ m\angle 1=150^{\circ }\)

Prove: \(m\angle 6=30^{\circ }\)

  1. Line \(a\) is parallel to line \(b\). That is given.
  2. Angle 1 equals \(150^{\circ}\)
  3. Since we know that the lines are parallel, then we can use same-side interior angles to determine that angle 6 is \(30^{\circ}\)

Example 1.1

Do the Measures Create a Triangle?

The previous proof just showed that the three angle measures for every triangle must always add to \(180^{\circ }\). By applying this, you can now verify if three given angles are able to create a triangle by checking to see if the sum of their measures is \(180^{\circ }\)

Decide if the following three angle measures can create a triangle if: \(m\angle 1=30^{\circ }, m\angle 2=90^{\circ }, m\angle 3=60^{\circ }\).

Solution:

Yes, the three measures create a triangle because the three angles make the triangle angle sum theorem true: \(30+90+60=180\).

The Triangle Exterior Angle Theorem

An exterior angle to a triangle is created by extending one side of the triangle to create a linear pair with its adjacent angle. It is important to understand the relationship of the exterior angle to its interior angles. The triangle exterior angle theorem states that the exterior angle of a triangle is equal to the sum of the triangle’s remote interior angles which are the two nonadjacent angles inside the triangle. The word remote means away from, such as ‘a remote location’ which means a faraway place, or a ‘remote control’ for a TV because it is used away from the TV. Examine this relationship below. First always decide which angle is exterior and which are its remote interiors.

Here, the exterior angle is \(\angle 1\) and its remote interior angles are \(\angle 3\) and \(\angle 4\) because they are far away from \(\angle 1\).

The triangle exterior angle theorem can be applied when two or more triangles are attached. The examples tab contains questions where you will apply this and other properties of angles to find missing values.

3. Equations That Are Parallel
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

To prove that two or more equations are parallel lines, first change the equations into slope-intercept form and then follow the steps from before:

  1. Show the slopes are equal.
  2. Show that the y-intercepts are different.

Both equations are in slope-intercept form. Now you can see that the slopes are the same and the y-intercepts are different so the lines must be parallel to each other.

Parallel

In the image there are two equations. In order to see if the equations are parallel, follow the following steps:

  1. Change \(-\dfrac {3} {4}x+y=-6\) into slope-intercept form.
  2. Compare the slopes. In this situation, the slopes are equal.
  3. Next, compare the \(y\)-intercepts. In this situation the \(y\)-intercepts are different.
  4. Since the slopes are equal and the \(y\)-intercepts are different, the equations are parallel.

Not Parallel

In the image there are two equations. In order to see if the equations are parallel, follow the following steps:

  1. Change \(y+8=8x\) into slope-intercept form.
  2. Compare the slopes. In this situation, the slopes are different.
  3. Since the slopes are different, the equations are not parallel.
  4. The \(y\)-intercepts in this situation do not matter, because the slopes are different.

4. Create a Parallel Line Using an Equation and One Point
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

If you know the equation of one line and have a coordinate on a second line, you can build an equation for a new parallel line.

How to Create an Equation from a Parallel Line and a Given Point:

  1. Identify the slope value from the given equation.
  2. Plug in only the values for the slope and the given (\(x,y)\) point into slope-intercept form. The \(y\)-intercept must remain a variable.
  3. Solve for the \(y\)-intercept value.
  4. Substitute the slope and the new \(y\)-intercept into \(y=mx+b\) .

5. Parallel Lines with Two Pairs of Coordinates
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

If you are given two pairs of coordinates, you can determine if they create parallel lines by creating both equations of the lines.

Steps to Determine if Two Pairs of Coordinates Create Parallel Lines

  1. Use the slope formula to find the slope between the first two points.
  2. Plug in the slope and either point for \((x,y)\) in order to solve for \(b\), the \(y\)-intercept.
  3. Write the new equation in slope-intercept form.
  4. Repeat the process for the second pair of coordinates.
  5. Compare the slopes and \(y\)-intercepts of the equations as usual to decide if lines are parallel.

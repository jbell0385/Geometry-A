1. Determine Perpendicular Lines on the Coordinate Plane
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

Perpendicular Lines

Perpendicular Lines are lines that intersect at exactly at \(90^{\circ }\), or at a right angle. They are labeled with a small box at the intersection. The symbol for perpendicular lines is \(\perp\). See it applied below.

Perpendicular Lines Not Perpendicular Lines

Perpendicular Lines intersect at four \(90^{\circ }\) angles. Here, line \(l\) is perpendicular to line \(m\) which is written \(l\perp m\).

These lines are not perpendicular because they do not intersect at \(90^{\circ }\) angles.

Equations of Perpendicular Lines

On the coordinate plane, equations of two perpendicular lines have a special relationship to each other. While parallel lines have equal slopes, perpendicular lines must have the product of their slopes equal to -1. An easy way to understand this is that their slopes are both opposites (one positive and one negative) and reciprocals (they are flipped the fractions to each other). Notice that the slopes are both opposites (one is positive and one is negative) and reciprocals (they are flipped fractions). If the slope is a whole number, remember to write a 1 as its denominator. Also, note that the product of the slopes is -1. Therefore, \(n\perp p\).

Prove Lines Are Perpendicular On the Coordinate Plane

Solution:

In order to prove that lines are perpendicular on the coordinate plane, first find the slopes of each line and then compare the slopes to each other. Always simplify fractions if possible. To find the slopes, choose two points on each line where lines cross the grid lines. Here, the slopes of the lines are \(\frac{3}{1}\) and \(\frac{-1}{3}\) which are opposite reciprocals and have a product of -1. Example 1.2

Describe Lines on the Coordinate Plane

Solution:

Always simplify fractions if possible. Since the slopes are not equal, the lines can not be parallel. The lines intersect which rules out parallel as well.

In addition, the slopes are not opposite reciprocals and do not have a product of -1. Since these attributes are not true, the lines can not be perpendicular.

The only option is that the lines are neither parallel nor perpendicular.

2. Determine Perpendicular Lines Using Two Equations or Two Points
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

Perpendicular Equations of Lines

To tell if two or more equations represent perpendicular lines, evaluate the values of the slopes. If the value of the slopes are opposite reciprocals which makes their product equal to -1, then the lines are perpendicular. Unlike parallel lines, the two \(y\)-intercepts can be equal because this could be the point of intersection where the two lines meet at a right angle. Below, slope-intercept form will be used to examine the relationship.

Since the slopes are opposite reciprocals, the equations will create perpendicular lines when graphed.

Perpendicular Lines Using Two Points

To determine whether lines are perpendicular when given the coordinates for two points, you must find the slope of each and decide if they are opposite reciprocals of each other. Again, unlike parallel lines, the two \(y\)-intercepts can be equal because this could be the point of intersection where the two lines meet at a right angle.

Examine this relationship in the Examples tab.

Example 2.1

Decide if Lines Are Perpendicular Using Equations

Decide if the equations create perpendicular lines. Show your work to defend your answer.

line v: \(y=\frac{3}{2}x-5\)

line w: \(y=-\frac{4}{3}x+4\)

Solution:

The slope values are not opposite reciprocals so their product is not -1. The lines are therefore not perpendicular.

Example 2.2

Manipulate Equations to Decide if Lines Are Perpendicular.

Decide if any of the following lines are perpendicular by using their equations. Show your work to defend your answer.

line \(m\): \(y=\frac{3}{4}x-2\)

line \(p\): \(y-\frac{4}{3}x=5\)

line \(r\): \(3y+4x=-2\)

Solution:

First, convert each equation into slope-intercept form. Line \(m\) is already in the correct format. Then decide which slopes meet perpendicular criteria.

Since line \(m\) and line \(r\) have opposite reciprocal slopes, \(m\perp r\). Line \(p\) and line \(m\) are reciprocals but are not opposites so they are not perpendicular. Note that line \(p\) and line \(r\) are opposites so they can not be parallel nor perpendicular lines.

3. Create Equations of Perpendicular Lines
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

When given the equation of one line, the equation of a perpendicular line can be created if one point on the new line is known. Use the following steps to find the equation of the perpendicular line.

Step 1: Identify the slope or \(m\) value from the given equation and find its opposite reciprocal. Be sure that the equation is in slope-intercept form (point-slope form can be used as well though it has not been reviewed here).

Step 2: Substitute the new slope value and the values for the variables of \(\left(x,y\right)\) into the slope-intercept formula. Keep the \(y\)-intercept written as \(b\) from the formula.

Step 3: Solve for the \(y\)-intercept, the \(b\).

Step 4: Use the new slope and \(y\)-intercept values only to create the equation of the perpendicular line.

Related to Example 3.1

How to Create An Equation for a Perpendicular Line When Given an Equation and a Coordinate.

Find an equation in slope-intercept form perpendicular to \(y=2x-5\) that passes through \((-6,1)\).

  1. Identify the slope or m value from the given equation and find its opposite reciprocal.
  2. Substitute the new slope and the values for the known coordinates into the slope-intercept equation. Drop the original b value.
  3. Solve for b, the \(y\)-intercept.
  4. Use the new slope and the new \(y\)-intercept value to build the equation of the perpendicular line.

Substitution
×

The substitution method is most useful for systems of 2 equations in 2 unknowns. The main idea here is that we solve one of the equations for one of the unknowns, and then substitute the result into the other equation.

  1. Solve one of the equations for either x = or y = .
  2. Substitute the solution from step 1 into the other equation.
  3. Solve this new equation.
  4. Solve for the second variable.

Example: Solve the following system by substitution

\(2x + 3y = 5\)

\(x + y = 5\)

  1. Step 1: Solve one of the equations for either x = or y = . We will solve the second equation for y.
    1. \(x + y = 5\)
    2. \(y = 5 - x\)
  2. Substitute the solution from step 1 into the second equation.
    1. \(2x + 3y = 5\)
    2. \(2x + 3(5 - x) = 5\)
  3. Solve this new equation.
    1. \(2x + 3(5 - x) = 5\)
    2. \(2x + 15 - 3x = 5\)
    3. \(-x + 15 = 5\)
    4. \(-x = 5 - 15\)
    5. \(x = 10\)
  4. Solve for the second variable.
    1. \(y = 5 - x\)
    2. \(y = 5 - 10\)
    3. \(y = -5\)
  5. The solution is: \((x, y) = (10, -5)\)

1. One Step Algebraic Equations
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

Defining an Equation

An equation states that two things are equal to each other, one on either side. It must use an equal sign “=”. Examine the following equation:

\(5 - 3 = 1 + 1.\)

This equation states that the value of the left side \((5 - 3)\) is equal to the value of the right side \((1 + 1)\), even though the two sides look different. After simplifying both sides, the result is that \(2 = 2\).

Equations in Algebra

In algebraic equations, a variable, often written as a letter, is used to represent an unknown quantity. Investigate this algebraic equation: \(5x-2=8\). This equation states that the value of the left side \((5x-2)\) is equal to the value of the right side \((8)\) even though they look different.

Solving One Step Equations

To solve an equation in Algebra, you must find the value of the unknown quantity that is represented by the variable. To do so, you must isolate the variable (get the variable alone) on one side of the equation.

Three important hints:

  1. Always write your steps vertically and be sure to line up your equal sign under the previous sign.
  2. Always remember to complete the opposite operation to BOTH sides of the equal sign. Whatever is changed on one side must be changed on the other.
  3. Always check your work by substituting your answer into the place of the variable in the original problem. If solved correctly, you will get the same value on both sides.

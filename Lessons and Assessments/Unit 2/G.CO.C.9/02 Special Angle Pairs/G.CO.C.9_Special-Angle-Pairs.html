1. Complementary and Supplementary Angles
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

Special Pairs of Angles

There are two types of angle pairs that have useful properties. They can be either adjacent when they share a vertex and one side or they can be nonadjacent. These two types are called complementary and supplementary angles. The angle addition postulate can be used to apply the properties.

Complementary Angles

Two angles whose measures add together to make \(90^{\circ }\). Each angle is called the complement to the other.

Adjacent and Complementary

Non-adjacent and Complementary

Supplementary Angles

Supplementary angles are two angles whose measures add together to make \(180^{\circ }\). Each angle is called the supplement to the other. When the supplementary angles are also adjacent, they are called a linear pair because together they create a line.

Example 1.1

List all of the pairs of complementary and supplementary angles.

The complementary angles are \(\angle VSW\) with \(\angle WSY\). No other angles add together to make \(90^{\circ}\).

The supplementary angles are \(\angle VSW\) with \(\angle WST\), \(\angle VSY\) with \(\angle YST\). No other angles add together to make \(180^{\circ}\).

Definition of Vertical Angles

Two angles that lie directly across from each other created by the intersection of only two lines. They must share a vertex but can not have any shared sides and their rays travel in opposite directions. Decide which angles are vertical in the diagram.

The vertical angle pairs are \(\angle EFJ\) with \(\angle HFG\), \(\angle EFH\) with \(\angle JFG\).

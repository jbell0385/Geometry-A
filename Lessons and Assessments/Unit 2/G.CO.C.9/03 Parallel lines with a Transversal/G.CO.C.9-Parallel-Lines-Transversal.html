1. Transversals
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

Transversals

A transversal is a line that intersects two or more coplanar lines (lines that lie on the same plane). There are eight angles formed by a transversal \(t\) and two lines \(l\) and \(m\). Each extra line will create an extra four angles. These lines can be parallel or not parallel.

Transversal C

Interior Angles vs. Exterior Angles

When a transversal intersects two lines, two different groups of angles are formed, interior angles and exterior angles.

Interior Angles

Interior angles lie between the two lines. Here, the interior angles are \(\angle 3,\angle 4,\angle 5\) and \(\angle 6\).

Exterior Angles

Exterior angles lie outside of the two lines. Here, the exterior angles are \(\angle 1,\ \angle 2,\ \angle 7\) and \(\angle 8\).

Special Angle Pairs

There are important pairs of angle that are also created by a transversal. Examine the following pairs of angles. Note that they can be created if lines are parallel or not parallel. Always identify the transversal before you begin to look for special angle pairs.

Corresponding angles

Corresponding angles lie on the same side of the transversal and in the same or corresponding position. Examine the corresponding angles in the diagram.

Alternate Exterior Angles

Alternate Exterior Angles lie outside of the two lines and on different or alternate sides of the transversal.

Alternate Interior Angles

Alternate Interior Angles lie between the two lines and on different or alternate sides of the transversal.

Same Side Interior Angles

Same Side Interior Angles (also called consecutive interior angles) lie between the two lines and on the same side of the transversal.

When lines are parallel with a transversal, special angle pairs have different relationships. The following relationships are only true if the two lines are parallel. We will examine these using conditional statements, or sentences written such that if the first requirement or condition is met, then the result will be true. Conditional statements are often written in if-then form. You will use the following diagram to examine the four types of angles.

Find Missing Angle Measures

Corresponding Angles Theorem

If the two lines are parallel, then corresponding angles are congruent because the angles lie in the same position and the lines travel in the same direction.

Notice an important relationship created by these relationships: there are only two different angle measurements in the diagram. Here, \(\angle 3\), \(\angle 6\), \(\angle7\) and \(\angle 8\) are congruent and \(\angle 2\), \(\angle 1\), \(\angle 4\) and \(\angle 5\) are congruent. The pattern is that the measurements switch back and forth when traveling from angle to angle. This will always be true with parallel lines. Try to apply this method on the examples tab.

Alternate Exterior Angles Theorem

If the two lines are parallel, then alternate exterior angles are congruent.

Alternate Interior Angles Theorem

If the two lines are parallel, then alternate interior angles are congruent.

Same Side Interior Angles Theorem

If the two lines are parallel, then same side (consecutive) interior angles are supplementary.

Example 2.3

Prove the Same Side Interior Angles Theorem

Here a paragraph proof is used to prove the theorem. First, state what is to be proven and then defend statements made by justification or the reasoning used.

Use the diagram to the left to prove the same side interior angles theorem which states if lines are parallel, then same side interior angles are supplementary. Linear pairs such as \(\angle 4\) and \(\angle 8\) are supplementary because they create a line, so \(m\angle 8+m\angle 4=180^{\circ }\). Since \(\angle 6\cong \angle 8\) by the corresponding angles theorem, substituting \(m\angle 6\) in the place of \(m\angle 8\) will result in \(m\angle 6+m\angle 4=180^{\circ }\). Therefore, same side interior angles must be supplementary.

Steps to Find Missing Values Using Algebra

Solve for the Variable

Follow the first three steps as before in order to find the value of the variable. Then complete the additional two steps in order to solve.

Step 1: Decide which line is the transversal.

Step 2: Decide which angles are interior and which are exterior.

Step 3: Decide what special type of angles are pictured.

Step 4: Apply the relationship of the special type of angles in order to create an equation. New special relationships can be either congruent or supplementary.

Step 5: Solve the equation. Substitute your answer in to check your algebra and to find angle measurements if necessary.

Click on the examples tab to see these steps in action.

Example 3.1

Apply Algebra With One Missing Angle

Find the value of the variable. Use the steps in order to solve. Then check your answer.

By the corresponding angles theorem, corresponding angles are congruent. The equation is \(2x-12=64\).

Solution:

Step 1: Decide which line is the transversal. Here, the transversal is k.

Step 2: Decide which angles are interior and which are exterior. Here, the \(64^{\circ }\) is interior and \(\left(2x-12\right)^{\circ }\) is exterior.

Step 3: Decide what special type of angles are pictured. (See lower image left) Since one angle is interior and one is exterior and since both are on the same side of the transversal, the angles are corresponding. They lie in the same location on the two different lines (directly on the right).

Step 4: Apply the relationship of the special type of angles in order to create an equation. By the Corresponding Angles Theorem, corresponding angles are congruent. The equation is\(2x-12=64\).

Step 5: Solve the equation.

Example 3.2

Find the measure of each angle. Use the steps in order to solve. Then check your answer.

Solution:

Step 1: Decide which line is the transversal. Here, the transversal is y.

Step 2: Decide which angles are interior and which are exterior. Here, the \(\left(2g+11\right)^{\circ }\) and \(\left(5g-27\right)^{\circ }\) are both interior.

Step 3: Decide what special type of angles are pictured. Since both angles are interior and both are on the same side of the transversal, the angles are same side (consecutive) interior.

Step 4: Apply the relationship of the special type of angles in order to create an equation. By the same side interior angles theorem, the angles are supplementary so they must add to \(180^{\circ }\). The equation is \(2g+11+5g-27=180\).

Step 5: Solve the equation.

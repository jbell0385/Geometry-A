Types of Angles
×

Types of Angles:

  1. Right Angle: An angle that is \(90^{\circ}\).
  2. Obtuse: An angle that is greater than \(90^{\circ}\).
  3. Acute Angle: An angle that is less than \(90^{\circ}\).
  4. Straight Angle: An angle that is \(180^{\circ}\).
Close
Congruence
×

Congruence is when two figures have the same shape and size.

  1. Congruent angles are angles that have the same degree measure.
  2. Congruent segments have the same length.
  3. Congruent shapes have both congruent angles and congruent segments.
Close
Converse
×

Switching the hypothesis and conclusion of a conditional statement. For example, the converse of "If it is raining then the grass is wet" is "If the grass is wet then it is raining." Note: As in the example, a proposition may be true but have a false converse.

Example: If an angle measures \(88^\circ\) , then it is acute.

If an angle is acute, then the angle measures \(88^\circ\) . (Not True)

2. The Angle Addition Postulate
Instruction Examples Video Self-Check

The angle addition postulate states that the measure of two combined angles is the sum of the measures of the two individual angles added together.

Conversely, using the angle addition postulate, one individual smaller angle can be found by subtracting the second smaller angle from the total sum.

Here, If \(D\) is in the interior of \(\angle ABC\), then the measure of \(\angle ABC\) is equal to the sum of the measures of \(\angle ABD\) AND \(\angle DBC\) so you can use the equation:

\(m\angle ABC = m\angle ABD + m\angle DBC\)

Area of Rectangles

Area is the amount of square units used to cover a two-dimensional surface. A basic way to find how many squares it takes to cover a rectangular figure is to break the figure into squares and then count the squares. A rectangle is a special type of parallelogram that has four right angles.

By counting the total number of separate squares, you can see that the area of the rectangle is \(12\) square units, also written as \(12\ units^2\). A faster and better way to calculate area of rectangular figures is to take its base (or length) and multiply it by how many rows it has, called its height (or width). Therefore, the formula to find the area of rectangles is:

\(Area=base\cdot height\)

Example 1.3

Find Areas of Complex figures

Find the area of the unshaded region. Then find the area of the shaded region.

Solution:

To find the area of the unshaded region:

  • Find the areas of each unshaded rectangle separately.
  • Then combine them through addition to get the total unshaded area. Note: Since the top left rectangle has all sides congruent, it is a square and the same value can be used for both the base and height.

To find the area of the shaded region:

  • Decide what creates it.
  • It is a full rectangle with smaller rectangles cut out from it.
  • Find the area of the large rectangle and subtract the total area of the unshaded region that you just calculated.

Example 1.4

Cost Application

You want to tile your bathroom floor. The cost of each tile is $3.75 per square foot. The dimensions of your rectangular bathroom are four feet by seven feet. How much will the tiles cost for the floor? What other considerations should you make for the total cost of installation?

Solution:

Draw a diagram to examine the problem.

Since you will tile the floor of the rectangular room, you must calculate how many tiles it will take to cover the entire floor. Therefore, the area of the floor must be found.

Find the cost. Since the cost of one square foot of tile is $3.75 and there are 28 square feet to cover, multiply to find the total cost.

Total\(\) Cost: \(\$3.75\cdot 28=\$105\)

The tile will cost $105 for the bathroom floor.

Other considerations include:

  • Cost of grout (the material that holds the tile down).
  • The amount of time it will take to lay each tile.
  • Whether you want to complete the project by yourself or pay to have a friend or professional help you.
  • Whether different stores or manufacturers have the tile that you would like for a better price.

2. Construction Project
Project Introduction RFP and Task Examples Rubric

Deciding to embark on a construction project can be an exciting and overwhelming experience. Consider the Eiffel Tower in Paris and all of the pieces that went into building such an iconic structure. From the initial designs to the people building the structure and the troubleshooting of the numerous issues throughout the process, all of this hard work comes together to create the final product.

Any size construction project can feel overwhelming. To help navigate through these aspects, most organizations hire a general contractor to take the lead. When looking for potential contractors, organizations create a Request for Proposals (RFPs) which outlines the project, specific needs and expectations, budget constraints, and any other important information.

People interested in becoming the general contractor will respond to a Request for Proposal by creating their own proposal, or bid, to meet the organization's needs. Usually the organization, now called the client, will receive multiple proposals from interested contractors before they decide who they will hire. They then choose the proposal that works best for them, and the project can begin!

In this unit, you will become a general contractor! You will receive a Request for Proposals (RFPs) and will respond by creating your own proposal to hopefully be granted the building contract. In the next tabs you'll find the information you need: an example of a proposal, websites you may use for research, and a template for creating your own proposal. Feel free to research on your own to find other potential sources of support, talk to some local contractors, or watch some videos online about this topic. Good luck and have fun!
